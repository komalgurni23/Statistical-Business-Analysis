-----------------------------------------
Hypothesis Test 1
One-Sample T-Test
-----------------------------------------

Null Hypothesis (H0):
The average sales are equal to the expected average sales.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
The average sales are significantly different from the expected average sales.

Test Used:
One-Sample T-Test

Decision Rule:
If p-value < 0.05, Reject H0
Else, Fail to Reject H0

-----------------------------------------
Hypothesis Test 2
Independent T-Test
-----------------------------------------

Groups Compared:
North Region vs South Region

Null Hypothesis (H0):
There is no significant difference between sales in North and South regions.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
There is a significant difference between sales in North and South regions.

Test Used:
Independent T-Test

Decision Rule:
If p-value < 0.05, Reject H0
Else, Fail to Reject H0

-----------------------------------------
Hypothesis Test 3
One-Way ANOVA
-----------------------------------------

Groups Compared:
North
South
East
West

Null Hypothesis (H0):
All regional sales have the same mean.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
At least one region has a different mean.

Test Used:
One-Way ANOVA

Decision Rule:
If p-value < 0.05, Reject H0
Else, Fail to Reject H0
